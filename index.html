FAQ

Do I get a degree from project ulim?

No degree is given here and nothing is accredited. However, the progress made on the courses and stats from going through the courses is kept in the student's profile, and this profile can be uploaded into ulim.

How do I get a job?

Our thinking is acquiring more internships gives you more experience, which in turn makes you more desirable to employers. We wanted to make an entire pipeline to support this. First, go through all of the courses here. Then, build projects, and get internships on ulim.

Are there grades?

We use a mastery learning approach similar to Khan Academy. Thus, we do not have grades, and we recommend students achieve mastery in each subject before moving onto the next, as these courses build on each other.

Are there any age requirements?

Nope. Age is just a number. Joking aside, as long as a student's math skills reach the recommended prerequisite knowledge they can charge valiantly into these courses!

Are you associated with MIT or Khan Academy?

Nope. We loved the format of Khan Academy courses, and wanted to implement these here to have a consistent learning experience from start to finish. Plus, they do have the best learning experience in the world. Additionally, MIT OCW content is under a Creative Commons License, so we are not directly affiliated with them, but their content is attributed throughout.

What about a college experience?

We have big plans for this in the future. Isn't a college experience simply young people living together in a specific location? Why not have the option to live anywhere, on a campus or not, with other people curious about the same things? More on this in the future.
